Customer Analytics

Project Overview

Segmented customers for an automobile parts manufacturer using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) methodology to enhance targeted marketing strategies. The analysis identified distinct customer segments enabling personalized marketing campaigns and improved customer retention initiatives.

Problem Statement

The automobile parts manufacturer needed to understand their customer base to allocate marketing resources effectively. Without customer segmentation, marketing campaigns were generic and failing to resonate with different customer types, resulting in low engagement rates and suboptimal ROI on marketing spend.

Solution & Approach

Implemented comprehensive RFM analysis to segment customers and guide marketing strategy:

  • Collected and cleaned customer transaction data from CRM system
  • Calculated Recency (days since last purchase), Frequency (number of transactions), and Monetary (total spend) metrics
  • Scored customers on 1-5 scale for each RFM dimension
  • Created RFM segments (Champions, Loyal Customers, At Risk, Lost, etc.)
  • Performed exploratory data analysis to understand segment characteristics
  • Developed targeted marketing strategies for each customer segment
  • Created visualizations to communicate segment profiles to marketing team

Process & Methodology

Data preparation involved cleaning transaction records and handling missing values. Calculated RFM metrics and applied quantile-based scoring method. Segmented customers into 11 distinct groups based on RFM scores. Conducted exploratory data analysis to profile each segment's behavior patterns and value to the business.

Results & Outcomes

Successfully segmented 15,000+ customers into actionable groups including 8% Champions (high value, highly engaged), 18% Loyal Customers, 22% Potential Loyalists, and 15% At Risk customers. Marketing team implemented segment-specific campaigns resulting in 25% increase in email engagement rates and 30% improvement in customer retention for at-risk segments.
