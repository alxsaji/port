Data Analysis

Project Overview

Analyzed online retail order data using SQL to extract business insights and identify opportunities for operational improvement. The analysis uncovered patterns in customer ordering behavior, seasonal trends, and product performance metrics to support strategic business decisions.

Problem Statement

The online retail business needed comprehensive analysis of their order database to understand sales trends, customer behavior, and inventory needs. Without systematic data analysis, the business lacked visibility into key performance metrics and struggled to make informed decisions about inventory management and marketing strategies.

Solution & Approach

Developed comprehensive SQL queries and analysis framework to extract actionable insights:

  • Designed and normalized database schema for efficient querying
  • Created complex SQL queries using JOINs, subqueries, and window functions
  • Analyzed sales trends across different time periods and product categories
  • Identified top-performing products and customer segments
  • Calculated key metrics including customer lifetime value, repeat purchase rate, and average order value
  • Developed automated reporting queries for regular business insights
  • Optimized query performance using indexes and query optimization techniques

Process & Methodology

Database design involved normalizing tables and establishing relationships between orders, customers, and products. Developed SQL queries for exploratory analysis including aggregations, grouping, and filtering. Created views for commonly used metrics and implemented stored procedures for complex calculations. Documented all queries and analysis methodology for reproducibility.

Results & Outcomes

Identified seasonal purchasing patterns with 40% spike in orders during Q4. Discovered that 20% of customers contributed to 65% of total revenue, enabling focused retention strategies. Found that customers who purchased within first 30 days had 3x higher lifetime value. Analysis led to data-driven inventory planning reducing stockouts by 25% and improving cash flow management.
