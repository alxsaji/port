Marketing Analytics

Project Overview

Analyzed customer purchase patterns at a coffee chain using market basket analysis to identify product associations and optimize product placement. The insights enabled strategic cross-selling recommendations and increased average transaction value through data-driven merchandising decisions.

Problem Statement

The coffee chain wanted to understand which products customers frequently purchase together to optimize store layout and implement effective cross-selling strategies. Without data-driven insights into purchase patterns, the business was missing opportunities to increase revenue through strategic product placement and bundling.

Solution & Approach

Implemented market basket analysis using KNIME and association rule mining techniques:

  • Collected and preprocessed transaction data from multiple store locations
  • Transformed data into transaction format suitable for association rule mining
  • Applied Apriori algorithm to identify frequent itemsets
  • Generated association rules with confidence, support, and lift metrics
  • Filtered rules based on minimum support (5%) and confidence (40%) thresholds
  • Visualized product associations using network graphs
  • Developed actionable recommendations for product placement and promotions

Process & Methodology

Data preprocessing involved cleaning transaction records and encoding products for basket analysis. Applied association rule mining to discover frequent itemsets and product combinations. Evaluated rules using support, confidence, and lift metrics to identify statistically significant associations. Created visualizations to communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders.

Results & Outcomes

Identified 25+ high-confidence product associations including strong relationships between coffee and pastry items. Discovered that customers who purchased specialty coffee were 3.5x more likely to buy artisan sandwiches. Recommendations led to 18% increase in cross-selling success rate and 12% growth in average transaction value after implementing optimized product placement.
