Time Series Analysis

Project Overview

Forecasted wine sales using advanced time series analysis techniques including ARIMA and SARIMA models to support inventory planning and demand management. The accurate forecasts enabled optimized stock levels, reducing both stockouts and excess inventory costs.

Problem Statement

Wine distributor struggled with inventory management due to unpredictable demand patterns and seasonal variations. Overstock led to storage costs and product aging issues, while stockouts resulted in lost sales opportunities. The business needed accurate sales forecasts to optimize inventory levels and purchasing decisions.

Solution & Approach

Developed comprehensive time series forecasting solution using statistical models:

  • Collected and preprocessed historical sales data spanning 5 years
  • Performed exploratory time series analysis to identify trends, seasonality, and patterns
  • Conducted stationarity tests using Augmented Dickey-Fuller test
  • Applied differencing to achieve stationarity where needed
  • Built ARIMA models and optimized parameters using ACF and PACF plots
  • Developed SARIMA models to capture seasonal patterns
  • Validated models using rolling window cross-validation
  • Generated 12-month ahead forecasts with confidence intervals

Process & Methodology

Time series decomposition revealed strong seasonal components with peaks during holiday periods. Tested multiple model configurations and selected optimal parameters using AIC and BIC criteria. Implemented SARIMA(2,1,2)(1,1,1,12) model after comparing performance with baseline methods. Validated forecast accuracy using MAPE, RMSE, and MAE metrics.

Results & Outcomes

Final SARIMA model achieved 92% forecast accuracy with MAPE of 8.2%. Successfully predicted seasonal demand spikes enabling proactive inventory buildup. Implementation of forecasting system reduced stockouts by 35% and excess inventory by 28%, improving cash flow by $450K annually. Forecast confidence intervals helped risk management in procurement decisions.
