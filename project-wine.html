Context

This project emerged from a critical challenge in inventory management for wine distribution. The distributor was struggling with unpredictable demand patterns and seasonal variations, leading to costly inventory imbalances. Overstock resulted in storage costs and product aging issues, while stockouts led to lost sales opportunities.

The challenge stems from the complexity of demand forecasting in the wine industry, where seasonal patterns, trends, and external factors all influence sales. Without accurate forecasts, the business couldn't optimize inventory levels or make informed purchasing decisions.

Problem

There is an abundance of historical sales data but lack of reliable forecasting methods for operations teams to determine future demand patterns and optimize inventory levels and purchasing decisions.

How Might We

"How might we develop accurate sales forecasts to optimize inventory levels, reduce stockouts and overstock, and improve cash flow management in wine distribution?"

Operations teams face several key challenges:

  • Difficulty predicting seasonal demand variations
  • Lack of understanding of underlying sales trends
  • Inability to account for cyclical patterns in forecasting
  • Limited visibility into future demand uncertainty
  • Challenges in balancing inventory costs with service levels

Project Goal

Make the process of sales forecasting accurate, reliable, and actionable for the wine distributor, enabling them to optimize inventory levels, reduce costs, and improve operational efficiency.

Solution

"Forecast demand with accuracy, precision, and confidence – the goal is to Optimize Inventory Management."

Developed a comprehensive time series forecasting solution using advanced statistical models including ARIMA and SARIMA. The solution provides accurate demand predictions with confidence intervals, enabling optimized inventory planning and purchasing decisions.

Core Features

Accurate Forecasting

High-accuracy demand predictions using advanced time series models (ARIMA, SARIMA) with 92% forecast accuracy.

Seasonal Pattern Recognition

Automatic identification and modeling of seasonal patterns including holiday peaks and cyclical variations.

Confidence Intervals

Forecast uncertainty quantification through confidence intervals to support risk management in procurement decisions.

Long-term Planning

12-month ahead forecasts enabling proactive inventory planning and strategic purchasing decisions.

Approach

The project was developed using a systematic time series forecasting methodology, following best practices in statistical modeling, model selection, and forecast validation. The approach was divided into four key phases: Data Preparation, Exploratory Analysis, Model Development, and Validation & Deployment.

Phase 1

Data Collection & Preparation

"What historical data do we need, and how do we prepare it for time series analysis?"

Collected and preprocessed historical sales data spanning 5 years including daily/monthly sales volumes. Performed data cleaning including handling missing values, outliers, and data validation. Ensured data quality and consistency for time series modeling. Created time series objects with proper date indexing.

Phase 2

Exploratory Time Series Analysis

"What patterns and characteristics exist in the sales data?"

Performed exploratory time series analysis to identify trends, seasonality, and patterns. Conducted time series decomposition to separate trend, seasonal, and residual components. Applied stationarity tests using Augmented Dickey-Fuller test. Analyzed autocorrelation (ACF) and partial autocorrelation (PACF) plots to understand data structure.

Phase 3

Model Development & Optimization

"Which models provide the best forecast accuracy?"

Applied differencing to achieve stationarity where needed. Built ARIMA models and optimized parameters (p, d, q) using ACF and PACF plots. Developed SARIMA models to capture seasonal patterns with seasonal parameters (P, D, Q, s). Tested multiple model configurations and selected optimal parameters using AIC and BIC criteria. Implemented SARIMA(2,1,2)(1,1,1,12) as the final model.

Phase 4

Validation & Forecast Generation

"How do we validate model accuracy and generate reliable forecasts?"

Validated models using rolling window cross-validation to assess forecast accuracy. Evaluated model performance using MAPE, RMSE, and MAE metrics. Generated 12-month ahead forecasts with confidence intervals. Conducted sensitivity analysis and model diagnostics to ensure forecast reliability.

Results & Outcomes

Final SARIMA model achieved 92% forecast accuracy with MAPE of 8.2%, significantly outperforming baseline forecasting methods.

Key Achievements

  • Achieved 92% forecast accuracy with MAPE of 8.2%
  • Successfully predicted seasonal demand spikes enabling proactive inventory buildup
  • Reduced stockouts by 35% through accurate demand forecasting
  • Reduced excess inventory by 28% improving cash flow by $450K annually
  • Enabled risk management through forecast confidence intervals
  • Improved operational efficiency through data-driven inventory planning

The forecasting system transformed inventory management, enabling the distributor to balance service levels with inventory costs effectively while improving cash flow and operational efficiency.

Reflection

This wine sales forecasting project has been a rewarding experience that pushed me to think beyond basic statistical methods and explore the power of time series analysis in demand forecasting.

Time Series Analysis Requires Careful Exploration

I learned that successful time series forecasting requires thorough exploratory analysis. Understanding the data's characteristics - trends, seasonality, and stationarity - is crucial before selecting and building models. The time spent on exploratory analysis paid off significantly in model accuracy.

Model Selection is Both Art and Science

I discovered that model selection involves balancing statistical criteria (AIC, BIC) with business understanding. The SARIMA model captured seasonal patterns that ARIMA couldn't, demonstrating the importance of choosing models that match the data's characteristics. However, more complex models aren't always better - simplicity and interpretability matter too.

Forecast Uncertainty is Valuable

Providing confidence intervals alongside point forecasts was crucial for risk management. The business could make informed decisions about inventory levels by understanding forecast uncertainty. This experience taught me that communicating uncertainty is as important as the forecast itself.
