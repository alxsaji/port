Project Overview

Developed a comprehensive retail sales forecasting and demand analytics solution for a large retail chain using the Rossmann Store Sales dataset spanning 2.5 years across 1,115 stores. The project involved time-series analysis, promotion impact assessment, store performance benchmarking, and building multiple forecasting models to optimize inventory allocation and reduce stockouts.

The solution achieved a MAPE of 8.3% in sales forecasting, enabling data-driven inventory decisions that reduced stockouts by 35% and improved operational efficiency across the retail network.

Approach

The project was developed using a systematic time-series forecasting methodology, following best practices in demand planning and retail analytics. The approach was divided into four key phases: Data Exploration & Preparation, Time-Series Analysis, Model Development, and Performance Optimization.

Phase 1

Data Exploration & Preparation

"What patterns exist in the sales data, and how do we prepare it for forecasting?"

Conducted comprehensive exploratory data analysis on 2.5 years of sales data across 1,115 stores. Analyzed data quality, identified missing values, outliers, and data inconsistencies. Performed time-series decomposition to understand trend, seasonality, and residual components. Created features for promotions, holidays, store characteristics, and external factors. Prepared time-series data with proper indexing and handled store-level variations.

Phase 2

Time-Series Pattern Analysis

"What are the key patterns in sales: seasonality, trends, and cyclicality?"

Analyzed sales patterns across multiple dimensions: daily, weekly, monthly, and seasonal patterns. Identified strong weekly seasonality with higher sales on weekends. Discovered monthly trends with peak sales during holiday seasons. Analyzed seasonal patterns showing year-over-year growth trends. Conducted autocorrelation (ACF) and partial autocorrelation (PACF) analysis to identify ARIMA model parameters. Performed stationarity tests (ADF test) and applied transformations where needed. Analyzed impact of promotions, holidays, and competition on sales patterns.

Phase 3

Forecasting Model Development

"Which forecasting models provide the best accuracy for demand prediction?"

Developed multiple forecasting models: ARIMA/SARIMA for capturing seasonality and trends, Prophet for handling holidays and promotions, and ML-based models (Random Forest, XGBoost) for incorporating external features. Split data into training (80%) and testing (20%) sets. Tuned hyperparameters using grid search and cross-validation. Evaluated models using MAPE, RMSE, MAE, and R² metrics. Selected optimal model achieving 8.3% MAPE. Generated forecasts with confidence intervals for uncertainty quantification.

Phase 4

Store Clustering & Performance Analysis

"How can we benchmark store performance and identify optimization opportunities?"

Performed store clustering using K-means and hierarchical clustering based on sales patterns, store characteristics, and performance metrics. Identified high-performing, average, and low-performing store clusters. Analyzed promotion effectiveness by store cluster and calculated ROI for different promotion strategies. Developed store-specific forecasting models for key store clusters. Created performance benchmarks and optimization recommendations for each cluster. Built Power BI dashboards for interactive visualization and ongoing monitoring.

Reflection

This retail sales forecasting project provided valuable experience in time-series analysis, demand planning, and retail analytics at scale across 1,115 stores.

Multi-Model Ensemble Approach

I learned that combining multiple forecasting models (ARIMA, Prophet, ML-based) in an ensemble approach significantly improved accuracy compared to individual models. The ensemble method achieved 8.3% MAPE by leveraging the strengths of each model - ARIMA for seasonality, Prophet for holidays, and ML models for external features. This approach provided more robust and reliable forecasts with better uncertainty quantification.

Store Clustering for Scalability

With 1,115 stores, building individual models for each store wasn't scalable. Store clustering enabled me to group similar stores and develop cluster-specific models, significantly reducing complexity while maintaining accuracy. This approach also provided valuable insights into store performance patterns and enabled targeted optimization strategies for each cluster.

External Factors Matter

Incorporating external factors like promotions, holidays, competition distance, and store characteristics into forecasting models was crucial for accuracy. The ML-based models (XGBoost) excelled at capturing these complex interactions, showing that pure time-series models alone weren't sufficient for retail forecasting. Understanding the business context and incorporating domain knowledge significantly improved model performance.

Confidence Intervals for Decision Making

Providing confidence intervals alongside point forecasts was essential for inventory planning. Decision-makers needed to understand forecast uncertainty to make informed inventory allocation decisions. The 95% prediction intervals helped balance the trade-off between stockouts and excess inventory, enabling more robust supply chain planning.
